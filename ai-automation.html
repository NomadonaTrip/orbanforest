What Is AI Automation?

AI automation is the combination of two things. First, a language model — usually GPT, Claude, or an equivalent — that can read, write, and reason the way a human would. Second, a workflow engine like n8n, Make, or Zapier that plugs the language model into the rest of your business: your inbox, your calendar, your CRM, your phone system, your website.

On its own, a chatbot answers questions. Paired with automation, it answers the question, books the appointment, sends the confirmation email, tags the lead in your CRM, and notifies you in Slack if something needs your attention. One moving part instead of five.

How We Work

We start with a free 30-minute audit of where your time is actually going. Usually three to five repetitive tasks show up quickly, and one of them is almost always worth automating first. From there:

  • Week 1 — Map. We document the workflow as it exists, identify the decision points, and agree on what "done" looks like.
  • Week 2–3 — Build. We wire up the automation, test it against real scenarios, and tune the language model's prompts until it behaves the way a good employee would.
  • Week 4 — Launch. It goes live behind a kill switch. You watch it for a week before handing over control.
  • After launch — Monitor. Every automation we build has logging. We review the first month of runs together and tune anything that needs tuning.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is AI automation just another chatbot?

No. A chatbot is one thin slice of AI automation. The bigger value is in connecting the AI to your calendar, CRM, phone, email, and business logic — so the system doesn't just answer, it acts.

Will this replace my staff?

In almost every case we've built, the answer is no. It replaces the boring part of their job — the 11 p.m. emails, the scheduling tetris, the quote follow-ups nobody has time for — so they can do the work that actually requires a human.

How long does it take to build?

A scoped first automation: two to four weeks. A full integrated system: six to ten weeks. We don't do six-month consulting engagements. If it takes longer than ten weeks, something's wrong with the scope.
